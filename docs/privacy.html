Privacy Policy

Effective date: 2026-05-17  ·  Last updated: 2026-05-17

1. Summary

Counter is a native iPad application for studio professionals — tattoo artists, removal specialists, and hairdressers — to manage their clients, bookings, finances, and portfolio. Counter is designed to keep all of your data, and your clients' data, on your device. We do not operate servers that store your business records, and we do not have access to the information you put into the app.

This policy explains what little information we do handle, what stays on your device, and your responsibilities when you use Counter to process information about other people (your clients).

2. Who this policy applies to

This policy describes the practices of Mickey Macdonald ("we", "us", or "Counter"), the developer of the Counter iPad application, based in Calgary, Alberta, Canada.

It applies to:

  • Visitors to thecounterapp.ca
  • People who contact us at mickey@thecounterapp.ca
  • Users of the Counter iPad app

It does not govern how you, as a Counter user, handle the personal information of your clients. When you enter client information into Counter, you are the data controller for that information and are responsible for complying with the privacy laws applicable to your business and jurisdiction. See Section 8 below.

3. Information we collect

3.1 Information you give us directly

If you email us at mickey@thecounterapp.ca or use the contact form on thecounterapp.ca, we receive whatever you choose to send us — typically your name, email address, and the contents of your message. We use this only to respond to you.

3.2 Information collected automatically by the website

thecounterapp.ca is hosted on GitHub Pages. GitHub may collect standard server logs (IP address, user agent, request time) for security and abuse-prevention purposes under GitHub's Privacy Statement. We do not run any analytics scripts on this site.

3.3 Information collected by the Counter iPad app

The Counter app does not transmit your data to us. Specifically:

  • Counter does not include any third-party analytics or telemetry SDK. No usage data, events, or metrics are sent anywhere outside of what Apple may collect through the App Store.
  • Counter does not send crash reports to us. Crash reports may be sent to Apple if you have opted in to "Share With App Developers" in iOS Settings; if so, Apple may share aggregated, anonymized crash data with us through App Store Connect.
  • Counter does not require an account, login, or sign-up.
  • Counter does not use advertising identifiers and does not display ads.

3.4 Information stored locally on your device

When you use Counter, the app stores the following on your iPad only:

  • Client records you enter, which may include names, contact information, and any health notes, intake responses, or session history you choose to record.
  • Booking and session data, including dates, times, prices, and notes.
  • Financial records, including payments, deposits, balances, and discounts.
  • Images you import into the gallery, sketches, stencils, reference images, and signed agreements.
  • Templates and settings you create.
  • Recovery backup files generated by the in-app recovery service. These backups are stored locally on your device only and are never transmitted to external servers or any cloud destination.

This information is stored in the app's local SwiftData store and is protected by iOS file-system encryption when your device is locked.

3.5 Biometric data

Counter offers Client Mode, which can be locked behind Face ID. Counter does not receive, see, or store your biometric data. Apple's Secure Enclave performs the Face ID match locally and only returns a yes/no answer to the app. No biometric data is ever logged or transmitted.

4. How we use information

We use the small amount of information we do receive (emails you send us) only to:

  • Respond to your support requests, feedback, or questions
  • Diagnose problems you report
  • Send you back a reply

We do not sell, rent, trade, or share your information with third parties for marketing purposes. We do not have a mailing list or newsletter.

5. Data sharing and third parties

We do not share information you send us except:

  • With service providers strictly necessary to operate the website or respond to email (for example, our email host and GitHub Pages), and only to the extent they need it to perform their function
  • When required by law, court order, or a valid legal process under Canadian law
  • To protect the rights, safety, or property of Counter, our users, or the public

The Counter app itself does not share data with third parties because it does not transmit data off your device.

6. Data retention

  • Emails you send us: retained for as long as we reasonably need them to provide support and a record of correspondence, then deleted.
  • Data you store in the Counter app: retained on your device until you delete it. Uninstalling the app removes its local SwiftData store and all associated app data from your device.

7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under PIPEDA (Canada), PIPA (Alberta or BC), the GDPR (EU/UK), or the CCPA (California), including the right to:

  • Know what personal information we hold about you
  • Request a copy of that information
  • Request correction of inaccurate information
  • Request deletion of your information
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Lodge a complaint with a privacy regulator

Because the only personal information we typically hold about you is correspondence you have voluntarily sent us, exercising these rights is usually as simple as emailing mickey@thecounterapp.ca.

For information you have entered into Counter about your own clients, we cannot help — that data is on your device and we have no access to it. Your clients should direct their requests to you, the studio operator.

8. Your responsibilities as a Counter user

If you use Counter to manage information about your clients, you are the data controller for that information under most privacy laws. That means:

  • You are responsible for collecting, storing, and using client information in compliance with the privacy laws applicable to your jurisdiction and profession.
  • You should obtain appropriate consent from your clients before recording sensitive information such as health notes or intake answers.
  • You are responsible for keeping your iPad physically secure and protected by a strong device passcode.
  • You are responsible for honoring requests from your clients to access, correct, or delete their information.
  • If your iPad is lost or stolen, you are responsible for any breach notification obligations that may apply to you.

Counter is a tool. We provide it; you are accountable for how you use it.

9. Children's privacy

Counter is intended for use by adult professionals. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in jurisdictions where that is the threshold). If you believe a child has sent us information, please contact us so we can delete it.

10. International users

Counter is developed in Canada. If you contact us from outside Canada, your message will be processed in Canada and possibly other jurisdictions where our service providers operate (notably the United States, where GitHub is based). Canadian and U.S. privacy laws may differ from those in your country.

11. Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the small amount of information we hold:

  • The website is hosted on GitHub Pages and does not collect or store personal information beyond what GitHub's platform logs.
  • Email correspondence is handled through our standard email provider. When you contact us via the contact form or directly, your message is delivered to a private inbox. We do not use third-party email routing services that process your messages.
  • The Counter app benefits from iOS sandboxing and file-system encryption when your device is locked.
  • Counter does not transmit your business data over the network automatically. All external communication initiated from the app is explicitly user-prompted: for example, the email template feature composes a draft and opens your Mail client for you to review and send; no messages are sent without your action. Similarly, PDF exports and shared documents are only sent when you initiate a share via the iOS Share Sheet.

No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we do, we will update the "Last updated" date at the top. Material changes will be noted on our website. Continued use of Counter or our website after a change indicates acceptance of the updated policy.
