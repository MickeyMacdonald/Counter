## 1. Summary

Counter is a native iPad application for studio professionals — tattoo artists, removal specialists, and hairdressers — to manage their clients, bookings, finances, and portfolio. **Counter is designed to keep all of your data, and your clients' data, on your device.** We do not operate servers that store your business records, and we do not have access to the information you put into the app.

This policy explains what little information we *do* handle, what stays on your device, and your responsibilities when you use Counter to process information about other people (your clients).

## 2. Who this policy applies to

This policy describes the practices of **Mickey Macdonald** ("we", "us", or "Counter"), the developer of the Counter iPad application, based in Calgary, Alberta, Canada.

It applies to:

- Visitors to **thecounterapp.ca**
- People who contact us at **mickey@thecounterapp.ca**
- Users of the **Counter iPad app**

It does **not** govern how *you*, as a Counter user, handle the personal information of *your* clients. When you enter client information into Counter, you are the data controller for that information and are responsible for complying with the privacy laws applicable to your business and jurisdiction. See Section 8 below.

## 7. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under PIPEDA (Canada), PIPA (Alberta or BC), the GDPR (EU/UK), or the CCPA (California), including the right to:

- Know what personal information we hold about you
- Request a copy of that information
- Request correction of inaccurate information
- Request deletion of your information
- Withdraw consent at any time
- Lodge a complaint with a privacy regulator

Because the only personal information *we* typically hold about you is correspondence you have voluntarily sent us, exercising these rights is usually as simple as emailing `mickey@thecounterapp.ca`.

For information *you have entered into Counter about your own clients*, we cannot help — that data is on your device and we have no access to it. Your clients should direct their requests to you, the studio operator.

## 8. Your responsibilities as a Counter user

If you use Counter to manage information about your clients, **you are the data controller** for that information under most privacy laws. That means:

- You are responsible for collecting, storing, and using client information in compliance with the privacy laws applicable to your jurisdiction and profession.
- You should obtain appropriate consent from your clients before recording sensitive information such as health notes or intake answers.
- You are responsible for keeping your iPad physically secure and protected by a strong device passcode.
- You are responsible for honoring requests from your clients to access, correct, or delete their information.
- If your iPad is lost or stolen, you are responsible for any breach notification obligations that may apply to you.

Counter is a tool. We provide it; you are accountable for how you use it.

## 9. Children's privacy

Counter is intended for use by adult professionals. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 in jurisdictions where that is the threshold). If you believe a child has sent us information, please contact us so we can delete it.
